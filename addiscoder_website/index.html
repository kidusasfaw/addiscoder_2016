Addis Coder Algorithms and Programming for High Schoolers

Addis Coder

This is the website for a summer course on algorithms and programming for high schoolers, in Addis Ababa from July 18th to August 12th, 2016. Here you will find lecture notes, lab assignments, and contact information for the instructors. You can begin by downloading Anaconda.

Instructors

Boaz Barak

Timnit Gebru

Jelani Nelson
Boaz Barak (b@boazbarak.org) is the Gordon McKay Professor of Computer Science at Harvard University. Timnit Gebru (tgebru@cs.stanford.edu) is a computer science Ph.D. student at Stanford university. Jelani Nelson (minilek@seas.harvard.edu) is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Harvard University.

Course Content

Week 1:

  • Day 1: Basic types, operations, and functions
  • Day 2: Control flow: for loops and if statements
  • Day 3: Review. Lab 1.5.
  • Day 4: Lists and more for loops, and more for loops examples
  • Day 5: While loops, and more while loop examples

Week 2:

  • Day 1: Functions I
  • Day 2: Functions II
  • Day 3: Function exercises
  • Day 4: Recursion
  • Day 5: Recursion exercises

Week 3:

  • Day 1: Solving linear systems [pdf]
  • Day 2: More linear system solving, sorting [pdf]; morning lab exercises [pdf]
  • Day 3: Algorithmic efficiency, MergeSort [pdf]
  • Day 4: binary search, dictionaries, hashing, graphics [pdf]
  • Day 5: graphs, queues, stacks, depth/breadth-first search [pdf]; quiz solutions

Week 4:

  • Day 1: Memoization
  • Day 2: More memoization, tracing the dynamic programming table
  • Day 3: Memoization on graphs: Bellman-ford
  • Day 4: Fast integer multiplication: Karatsuba's algorithm
  • Day 5: Final exam and course wrap-up
